The Artist

As both a scientist studying the brain and an artist, I've developed a deep appreciation for art and the human visual perception that surrounds it. When we view art, both objective and subjective thoughts arise. I would describe the pleasure of painting as a process that begins with an idea which emerges through dimensions and color. Within this gradual creative process, the artist's emotions are then released into the work and its composition. Art is never without an audience. We all bring diverse backgrounds and perspectives that shape how we perceive art and the emotions and opinions we form about each piece. Through sharing my art, I hope to express my passion for human behavior, animal behavior, and our connection with nature. We live alongside other organisms that express complex social relationships similar to humans, and part of my artistic expression involves bringing to life the behavioral concepts and social relationships I admire. My favorite aspect of being an artist is hearing others' creative thoughts and imaginative minds, and sharing in these playful exchanges.

My work represents my interest in the rules behind social behavior "nomos" and my exploration of these behavioral patterns not only in humans, but also in other animals and non-human primates.

My Studio

My art studio is nothing special, just a chair that helps me maintain good posture, some good lighting, and paint and art supplies that appear chaotically organized from afar (though I've developed some spatial memory for where everything is). I enjoy reclaiming and buying supplies from family-owned art shops, as well as finding materials at thrift stores or out in nature, like collecting red clay. My creative process must be approved by my two cats (Charlie the orange cat & Tycho the tuxedo cat), who enjoy sitting beside me and inspecting my equipment to ensure it meets their feline standards.
